# KINGrinder K6 — Grinder Research

## TL;DR

- **Hardware spec:** 48 mm heptagonal stainless-steel conical burrs, external adjustment, 60 clicks/rotation, **16 µm/click** (manufacturer). Total useful range ≈ 3 rotations (180 clicks), with HCG's charts showing settings up to ~2.40 (160 total clicks).
- **HCG back-fit disagrees.** HCG places V60 at 0.48–1.22 (48–82 clicks), espresso at 0.22–0.45 (22–45 clicks), moka at 0.43–1.18 (43–78 clicks). Solving `µm = zeroOffset + micronsPerClick × click` across espresso/moka/V60 gives **~8.5 µm/click with a ~40 µm factory zero offset**, not 16 µm/click. Same shape of discrepancy as 1Zpresso K-Ultra (20 vs 7.6) and Timemore C2 (80 vs 35) — follow HCG.
- **Recommendation:** `micronsPerClick: 8.5, zeroOffset: 40, minClick: 0, maxClick: 180, dialNotation: 'numbered'`. No excluded methods; espresso is supported. No sibling variants share this config.
- **Dial notation:** `R.CC` — e.g., `1.22` = 1 full rotation + 22 clicks = 82 total clicks from zero.

## Key findings

### Hardware

- **Burrs:** 48 mm heptagonal stainless-steel conical. 7 cutting edges. KINGrinder's comparison page lists the K6 as "Heptagonal stainless steel burr, versatile, handles light roasts well." Visually similar to 1Zpresso's K-series heptagonal burr but with subtle differences in lower-blade geometry (noted by Brew Coffee Home's side-by-side vs. K-Ultra).
- **Adjustment:** External collar, 60 clicks per rotation, 16 µm/click per manufacturer. Dial reads `Rotation.Clicks`, e.g., `2.42` = 2 rotations + 42 clicks = 162 total clicks. HCG's published ranges run up to `2.40` (160 clicks); some reviewers report a physical maximum of 3–4 rotations (180–240 clicks).
- **Manufacturer µm/click:** 16 µm. Total range if accepted: 180 × 16 = 2880 µm — far larger than HCG's "0 – 1350 µm" claim and inconsistent with where HCG places brew methods on the dial.
- **Zero-point:** Rotate the adjustment nut fully clockwise until resistance (burr contact). Some unit-to-unit variation reported — a small factory zero offset is likely. HCG's espresso low end at click 22 with METHOD_MICRON_RANGE lo at 210 µm implies a non-zero intercept (see back-fit below).
- **Capacity:** 25–35 g hopper. Aluminum body. Available in straight-handle and bent-handle variants (cosmetic only, same burr and mechanism).
- **Espresso:** Fully supported. Espresso is the fine end of the dial (0.22–0.45 per HCG). Bean Ground and We Are The Mouth confirm espresso is achievable but note "light-roast espresso is significant physical effort."

### Back-fit µm/click

Truth ranges from `METHOD_MICRON_RANGES`. Fitting `slope = (µm_hi − µm_lo) / (click_hi − click_lo)` per reliable method:

| Method | µm range | Click range | Slope (µm/click) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Espresso | 210–390 | 22–45 | 180/23 = **7.83** |
| Moka | 420–720 | 43–78 | 300/35 = **8.57** |
| V60 | 450–750 | 48–82 | 300/34 = **8.82** |
| AeroPress | 360–1050 | 38–113 | 690/75 = **9.20** |

Average of espresso/moka/V60: **(7.83 + 8.57 + 8.82) / 3 = 8.41 µm/click**. AeroPress excluded from average (wide range = noisy anchor).

**Intercept (zeroOffset)** from low ends: `zeroOffset = µm_lo − slope × click_lo`
- Espresso: 210 − 8.41 × 22 = 210 − 185 = **25**
- Moka: 420 − 8.41 × 43 = 420 − 362 = **58**
- V60: 450 − 8.41 × 48 = 450 − 404 = **46**

Average intercept ≈ **(25 + 58 + 46) / 3 = 43 µm**.

**Chosen values: `micronsPerClick = 8.5, zeroOffset = 40`** (rounded to nearest 0.5 / 5, within the noise floor). Verification:

| Method | Predicted clicks | HCG clicks | Δ |
|---|---|---|---|
| Espresso | (210−40)/8.5=20, (390−40)/8.5=41 → [20, 41] | [22, 45] | lo +2, hi −4 |
| Moka | (420−40)/8.5=45, (720−40)/8.5=80 → [45, 80] | [43, 78] | lo +2, hi +2 |
| V60 | (450−40)/8.5=48, (750−40)/8.5=84 → [48, 84] | [48, 82] | lo 0, hi +2 |
| AeroPress | (360−40)/8.5=38, (1050−40)/8.5=119 → [38, 119] | [38, 113] | lo 0, hi +6 |
| French | (780−40)/8.5=87, (1200−40)/8.5=137 → [87, 137] | [82, 154] | lo +5, hi −17 |
| Cold | (900−40)/8.5=101, (1200−40)/8.5=137 → [101, 137] | [95, 160] | lo +6, hi −23 |
| Chemex | (750−40)/8.5=83, (1020−40)/8.5=115 → [83, 115] | (not listed) | consistent with coarser-than-V60 |

Espresso/moka/V60 land within 0–4 clicks of HCG. AeroPress lo is exact. French and cold brew show divergence at the coarse end — HCG extends further right on the dial than the linear model predicts, probably because the heptagonal burr geometry produces progressively larger particle-size jumps per click at coarser settings (non-linear in practice). HCG overrides used for those methods.

**Total at maxClick 180:** 40 + 8.5 × 180 = 1570 µm — close to HCG's "0–1350 µm" claim (which likely reflects the usable range up to ~160 clicks, not a hard physical stop).

### Hardware-vs-HCG discrepancy

The 16 µm/click manufacturer spec implies 180 × 16 = 2880 µm total range. HCG's "0–1350 µm" and brew-method placements both contradict this. Back-fit gives 8.5 µm/click — approximately half the manufacturer's figure. This is the same pattern as the 1Zpresso K-Ultra (manufacturer 20 µm, HCG back-fit 7.6 µm) and Timemore C2 (manufacturer ~80 µm, HCG back-fit ~35 µm). The likely explanation: manufacturer figures describe mechanical thread pitch / vertical burr travel, not effective particle-size change per click. We follow HCG.

## Recommended config

```js
king_k6: {
  id: 'king_k6',
  name: 'KINGrinder',
  model: 'K6',
  subtitle: 'K6 · Heptagonal 48mm',
  minClick: 0,
  maxClick: 180,
  // 8.5 µm/click + 40 µm zero offset back-fits HCG's K6 brew ranges
  // across espresso/moka/V60/aeropress within 0–4 clicks. Manufacturer's
  // 16 µm/click figure implies a >2800 µm range inconsistent with HCG's
  // "0–1350 µm" claim and brew-method placements. Same discrepancy shape
  // as K-Ultra (20 vs 7.6) and C2 (80 vs 35) — follow HCG.
  micronsPerClick: 8.5,
  zeroOffset: 40,
  majorTick: 60,       // one major tick = one full rotation; dial reads R.CC
  accentColor: '#a07850',
  dialNotation: 'numbered',
  methodOverrides: {
    french: [82, 154],   // HCG: 1.22–2.34; linear model lo is 5 clicks coarse of HCG
    cold:   [95, 160],   // HCG: 1.35–2.40; linear model lo/hi both 6–23 off at coarse end
  },
}
```

## Caveats

- **maxClick = 180** is conservative (3 rotations). HCG's coldest method tops at ~160 clicks (2.40). Brew Coffee Home reports a physical max of 240 (4 rotations), but nothing useful lives above 160. Setting maxClick to 180 gives a small buffer.
- **majorTick = 60** (one full rotation) matches the dial's physical notch at each full rotation. The `dialNotation: 'numbered'` flag causes the UI to render `R.CC`-style labels. Within a single rotation the existing 10-sub-click rendering doesn't apply here — the K6 has 60 clicks per rotation, not 10. The majorTick value drives the tick-mark density on the dial arc; 60 is correct.
- **No excluded methods.** Espresso is supported (HCG: 22–45 clicks); heptagonal burrs work for all brew methods.
- **Chemex not listed by HCG.** Linear model gives [83, 115] clicks, which sits naturally between V60 (48–82) and French press (82–154) — geometrically correct. methodOverrides not needed.
- **French and cold brew use methodOverrides** because the linear model diverges noticeably at the coarse end (likely non-linear gap expansion per click).

## Do not confuse with

- **KINGrinder K4** — exterior adjustment, 60 clicks/rotation, 16 µm/click mechanical, but **titanium-coated burr** (not uncoated stainless). Different burr surface. Likely needs a separate config if/when added.
- **KINGrinder K2 / K3 / K5** — interior adjustment models, 40 clicks/rotation, 18 µm/click, 48 mm burr. Completely different mechanism; not covered by this config.
- **KINGrinder K0 / K1** — interior adjustment, 38 mm burr, 40 clicks/rotation. Entry-level; different config.
- **1Zpresso K-Ultra** — also 48 mm heptagonal conical, also has an external adjustment. Different manufacturer, different burr (no base ring), different step size (7.6 µm/click back-fit). Already in the calibrator — do not merge.
