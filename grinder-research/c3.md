# Timemore Chestnut C3 / C3S — Grinder Research

## TL;DR

- **Hardware:** 38 mm stainless **S2C660** spike-to-cut conical burrs (the C3 generation's distinguishing burr — different from the C2's "Standard"). Single un-numbered ring, **12 clicks per rotation**, ~25 clicks of useful range per HCG, 0–950 µm total. C3 (2021) and C3S (2023, all-metal/silicone-base refresh) share identical burrs, dial, click count and HCG brew-method placements, so one config covers both.
- **Manufacturer hardware figure:** ~83 µm/click (Brew Coffee Home, Timemore burr-resolution pattern). HCG back-fit puts it at **~41 µm/click** — exactly half — with `zeroOffset = 0` (burrs-touching zero, same as the C2). Same shape as the C2 discrepancy.
- **Recommended config:** `micronsPerClick: 41, zeroOffset: 0, minClick: 0, maxClick: 25, majorTick: 5`. No `dialNotation` (single un-numbered ring). No `excludedMethods` — HCG places espresso at 5–10. `variants: 'C3/C3S'`.
- **Do not confuse with C3 ESP / C3 ESP Pro / C3 Pro.** Those are espresso-tuned variants with a 30 click/rotation CNC dial, ~23 µm/click, multi-rotation travel. Different mapping entirely.

## Key findings

### Hardware

- **Burrs:** 38 mm stainless **S2C660** spike-to-cut conical. Smaller-feed geometry vs the C2's "Standard" burr; faster on filter, less fines on coarse settings. Identical between C3 and C3S (Brew Coffee Home: *"the C3S uses the same dial as the original C3 series, with 12 clicks of movement"*; Slow Pour Supply C-series comparison agrees). The C3S (2023) drops the C3's plastic adjustment ring for an aluminium one and adds a silicone base — internals are unchanged.
- **Adjustment:** Single un-numbered stepped ring. **12 clicks per full rotation** (Brew Coffee Home; Timemore product page). Useful range runs ~2 rotations to the coarse end.
- **Manufacturer µm/click:** ~83 µm (Brew Coffee Home, framing the C3 ESP's 23 µm step as more precise than *"the less accurate 83 microns of the C3S"*). Timemore does not put a µm/click figure on the C3 product page itself — same situation as the C2.
- **Zero-point:** Burrs-touching. Turn the adjustment dial fully clockwise until the crank stops; that's click 0. No factory gap (unlike the S3's 75 µm pre-set). HCG explicitly warns *"avoid using settings below 6 clicks, as these settings may dull the burrs"* — exactly the same language as on the C2 page.

### HCG C3 / C3S ranges

Source: <https://honestcoffeeguide.com/timemore-c3-grind-settings/> and <https://honestcoffeeguide.com/timemore-c3s-grind-settings/>. The two pages list **identical** click ranges across every method, confirming a single shared config.

| Method | Click range |
|---|---|
| Turkish | 2–5 |
| Espresso | 5–10 |
| Moka pot | 10–17 |
| AeroPress | 9–25 |
| V60 | 11–18 |
| Pour-over | 11–24 |
| French press | 19–25 |
| Cold brew | 22–25 |

Total grind range stated as 0–950 µm. Chemex is not published (consistent with HCG's C2 page).

### Back-fit µm/click

Truth ranges from `METHOD_MICRON_RANGES`. With `zeroOffset = 0`, solve `µm = micronsPerClick × click` per method using mid-of-band ↔ mid-of-band:

| Method | µm mid | HCG click mid | implied µm/click |
|---|---|---|---|
| Espresso | 300 | 7.5 | 40.0 |
| Moka | 570 | 13.5 | 42.2 |
| V60 | 600 | 14.5 | 41.4 |
| AeroPress | 705 | 17 | 41.5 |
| French | 990 | 22 | 45.0 (clamps at 25) |
| Cold | 1050 | 23.5 | 44.7 (clamps at 25) |

The four reliable methods (espresso, moka, V60, AeroPress) cluster at **40–42 µm/click**. French press and cold brew skew higher because HCG hard-clamps both at click 25 (the dial limit) — the same clamping artefact seen on the K-Ultra at 100. Picking **`micronsPerClick = 41, zeroOffset = 0`** and re-checking against HCG:

| Method | Truth µm | Predicted clicks | HCG clicks | Δ |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Espresso | 210–390 | 5–10 | 5–10 | exact |
| Moka | 420–720 | 10–18 | 10–17 | hi +1 |
| V60 | 450–750 | 11–18 | 11–18 | exact |
| AeroPress | 360–1050 | 9–26→25 | 9–25 | clamp matches |
| Chemex | 750–1020 | 18–25 | (not published) | — |
| French | 780–1200 | 19–29→25 | 19–25 | clamp matches |
| Cold | 900–1200 | 22–29→25 | 22–25 | clamp matches |

Every HCG-published method lands within 1 click. **41 µm/click is the empirical figure.**

### Hardware-vs-HCG discrepancy

Brew Coffee Home cites Timemore's hardware figure of **~83 µm/click**, which would imply the 25-click usable travel covers ~2080 µm — far past HCG's 0–950 µm. Halving the manufacturer's number lands precisely on HCG's brew-method placements. Same pattern as the C2 (Timemore says 80 µm/click, HCG back-fits ~35) and the K-Ultra (1Zpresso says 20 µm/click, HCG back-fits ~7.6). We use **41**.

## Recommended config

```js
timemore_c3: {
  id: 'timemore_c3',
  name: 'Timemore Chestnut',
  model: 'C3',
  variants: 'C3/C3S',
  subtitle: 'C3 · S2C660',
  minClick: 0,
  maxClick: 25,
  // 41 µm/click empirically back-fits HCG's C3/C3S brew-method ranges across
  // espresso/V60/moka/aeropress to within 1 click. Brew Coffee Home cites
  // Timemore's ~83 µm/click hardware figure, but that implies a >2 mm range —
  // HCG caps the dial at 950 µm. Same pattern as the C2 (80 hw vs 35 back-fit).
  micronsPerClick: 41,
  zeroOffset: 0,
  majorTick: 5,
  accentColor: '<pick distinct from existing>',
}
```

## Caveats

- **Coarse-end clamping.** With `micronsPerClick = 41` and 25 clicks, the C3 tops out at ~1025 µm. French press (truth max 1200) and cold brew (1200) clamp at click 25 — exactly what HCG itself does. Reality, not a bug.
- **Fine-end warning.** HCG: *"avoid using settings below 6 clicks, as these settings may dull the burrs."* The calibrator's espresso band starts at click 5 (matching HCG), one click into the discouraged zone. Worth noting but doesn't justify excluding espresso — HCG itself publishes 5–10.
- **The 41 µm/click figure is back-fit, not measured.** Same epistemic status as the C2's 35 and the K-Ultra's 7.6. Treat ±2 µm as uncertainty.
- **Single un-numbered dial.** Like the C2, every adjustment requires re-zeroing and counting. No `dialNotation: 'numbered'`.
- **C3 vs C3S are mechanically identical** for our purposes. The C3S is a 2023 build refresh (aluminium ring, silicone base, no plastic adjustment parts) — same S2C660 burrs, same 12-clicks/rotation dial, same HCG ranges. One config, `variants: 'C3/C3S'`.

## Do not confuse with

- **Timemore C3 ESP** — espresso-tuned. 30 clicks per rotation CNC dial, ~23 µm/click, multi-rotation travel (HCG's C3 ESP page reads dial as `R.N.T` over 3 rotations). Same S2C660 burrs but a completely different dial mechanism. **Separate config required if added.**
- **Timemore C3 ESP Pro** — further refinement of the C3 ESP with redesigned dial cap. Same ~23 µm step. **Separate config.**
- **Timemore C3 Pro** — C3 with metal upgrades (older naming). Treat as a C3 variant only after confirming dial pitch matches; if it ships with the ESP-style 30-click dial it belongs with the ESP family, not here.
- **Timemore C2 / C2 Max / C2S / C2 Fold** — already in the calibrator. Different burrs ("Standard" not S2C660), different µm/click (35 back-fit), 36-click dial. Separate entry (`timemore_c2`).
- **Timemore Chestnut X / S3** — 42 mm S2C890 burrs, multi-numbered dial, factory zero gap. Different class.
